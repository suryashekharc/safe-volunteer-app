Confidentiality Agreement

This Confidentiality Agreement covers the services provided by The SAFE Alliance ("SAFE") and the information shared by clients, as well as information designated by SAFE as sensitive information, including confidential business or personnel matters. The confidentiality agreement also includes photography and video, which may not be taken or shared unless it is done as part of the services provided and a SAFE Alliance-approved consent form has been signed.

Any information concerning the identity of individuals served by SAFE is to be kept confidential at all times, except as otherwise specified below.

SAFE may be required to share confidential information when:

SAFE is bound by law to report suspected child abuse, elder abuse and/or the abuse of a person with a disability; homicide, homicidal or suicidal threats. Staff is authorized to comply with the law by making all required reports.

A SAFE client signs a specific authorization to release information and the authorization is in the client's file.

SAFE may need to comply with court orders and properly issued subpoenas.

SAFE staff are bound by state law requirements to report abusive, illegal or sexually exploitive acts by other mental health professionals or organizations.

SAFE has video cameras installed on its campuses and in SAFE-owned vehicles for the safety and security of clients, staff, and visitors. SAFE does not share video recordings except as required by law or if SAFE suspects illegal activity. To protect privacy and confidentiality, SAFE does not allow audio/video recording or photos of other clients or staff on personal devices.

If necessary for a client's benefit, client information may be shared among SAFE staff or volunteers to assist in service provision. If there is a public health emergency and SAFE has knowledge that you may have been exposed to a communicable disease, SAFE may be required by law to report your name and contact information to public health authorities.

I have read, understood, and agree it is my legal responsibility to comply with this Confidentiality Agreement while at SAFE and after ending my association with SAFE.

Liability Release

I understand that while working at SAFE I may come into contact with infections and contagious diseases such as hepatitis B, salmonella, ringworm, tuberculosis, pink-eye, AIDS, and other types of diseases. I understand that SAFE may not be aware that some of the residents have a disease, and that, even if the staff is aware, they may not tell other persons due to privacy concerns. I also understand that SAFE is not undertaking any duty to determine whether a resident has a disease.

I also understand that it is my personal choice as to the precautions I wish to take. While I understand that SAFE is in no way undertaking the responsibility for advising me as to appropriate precautions to take, I am aware that health officials recommend wearing disposable gloves (which are supplied at the shelter) while cleaning up spills of blood, bloody saliva, urine, feces, or vomit, and using a mouthpiece for mouth-to-mouth resuscitation.

I have made the choice to be here and I will not hold SAFE liable for health problems I may incur while at any SAFE facility even if they are caused in whole, or in part, as a result of the negligence of a staff member or resident.

Mutual Commitment Form & Physical Intervention Policy

SAFE agrees to:

  • Provide quality training to volunteers
  • Provide support and assistance to volunteers
  • Furnish supplies and facilities to enable volunteers to function effectively
  • Provide an agency policy regarding volunteer responsibilities and expectations

As a volunteer for SAFE, I agree to:

  • Make a commitment of at least 8 hours per month for a minimum of 6 months beginning after all training is completed (some programs require a larger time commitment).
  • Affirm that information provided in this application is true and correct to the best of my knowledge.
  • Inform SAFE if I am named in complaints or indictments or convicted of offenses.
  • Be open to on-going suggestions for improvement from staff.
  • Participate in any and all training required by programs.
  • Adhere to the confidentiality policy.
  • Adhere to emergency, security, and shift procedures specified for the program site.
  • Maintain appropriate boundaries with clients.
  • Never allow family, friends, or anyone else on SAFE premises without prior approval of my volunteer supervisor or other authorized SAFE staff.
  • Never carry a firearm or other type of weapon on any SAFE premises.
  • Attend required volunteer support meetings or continuing education classes.
  • Notify my Volunteer Supervisor if I will be unavailable to volunteer for a given period of time.
  • Understand that if I miss more than 2 shifts and fail to notify staff, my volunteer status may be terminated.
  • Accept the Volunteer Director's final decision as to my involvement with SAFE.
  • Take any questions regarding SAFE policies, staff decisions, or the performance or conduct of another volunteer to my supervisor.
  • Understand that SAFE reserves the right to terminate volunteer assignments at any time.

I realize that breaching this agreement may be cause for my immediate dismissal.

Volunteer Physical Intervention Policy

I understand that as a volunteer with SAFE:

  • I will let staff handle aggressive situations.
  • If I am volunteering at the SAFE Children's Shelter campus, I will go to another cottage or locate staff to request help when needed.
  • I will move away from any aggressive situation.
  • If a resident is being aggressive, I will engage the other youth who are not involved and encourage them to move away from the crisis situation.
  • I will not place my hands on a resident to contain, restrain, or otherwise prohibit the movement of a resident.
  • I will not contain, restrain, or otherwise prohibit the movement of a resident for any reason, nor may I assist staff in doing so.

I understand that as a volunteer I may not ever put hands on a resident during a conflict situation. I cannot physically intervene with any children/youth receiving services by SAFE. I will let trained staff manage any crisis situations and will not physically engage for any reason, even if asked to do so by staff. If I physically intervene during a crisis with any resident, I acknowledge that I could lose my placement as a volunteer. I will contact Volunteer Services Staff if I witness a crisis or otherwise significant incident or am asked to do something I was trained not to do.
